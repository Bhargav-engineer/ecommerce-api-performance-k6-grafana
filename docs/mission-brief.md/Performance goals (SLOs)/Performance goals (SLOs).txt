Goal: Define 2–3 SLOs that are realistic and structured like real SRE teams use: percentile latencies and success rates over a time window, not “always fast.”

Industry examples commonly define SLOs like “p95 under 300 ms for 95–99% of requests” and “99.9% of requests succeed.”

Step 1 – Choose key flows (5 minutes)
Pick 2–3 flows:
Product browse/search.
Product details.
Checkout.

Step 2 – Define SLI types
For each flow, define:
Latency SLI: percentile for response time (p95, optionally track p99).
Availability / success rate SLI: % of successful responses (non‑5xx).
Optional: Resource utilization SLI: CPU usage on app container.

Step 3 – Turn into concrete SLOs
Use this simple template from SRE/SaaS practices:
“For the checkout endpoint, over a rolling 7‑day window:
At least 95% of requests complete in under 300 ms (p95 latency SLO).
At least 99% of requests succeed (no 5xx errors).
Under target load, average CPU usage on the API container remains below 70%.”

For browse:
“For browse/search endpoints, over a rolling 7‑day window:
At least 95% of requests complete in under 250 ms.
At least 99.5% of requests succeed.”
